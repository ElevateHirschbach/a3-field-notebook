Background Brief
Current state — as of early March 2026
What Expedited is supposed to be
  • A slip-seat relay shuttle — two drivers meet in the middle, swap trailers, and each returns home the same day
  • Every driver starts and ends at their home yard. No one lives in the truck.
  • The truck never stops moving: AM and PM departure waves, two full round trips daily per truck
  • Designed to be 100% self-contained — no OTR support, no local truck feeding the shuttle, no borrowed freight
  • Financial thesis: drivers home nightly → lower turnover. Truck runs 2 shifts → 2× asset utilization vs. standard OTR
  • Long-term: relay model runs consistent routes, known lanes, fixed swap points — the exact profile needed for autonomous trucks when Aurora technology comes online

Core Tension
What we were sold vs. what we actually built
What it was supposed to be
  • Drop-and-hook. Take-or-pay freight.
  • 100% self-contained — no OTR support
  • Consistent volume, both directions, 7 days a week
  • 5,600 miles / truck / week
  • Plug-and-play ready for autonomous trucks
  • No "feeding the shuttle" from other divisions
What it actually is
  • Mixed freight — dedicated + whatever fits
  • OTR trucks staging loads to feed the shuttle
  • Loads that don't fit the model forced in for utilization
  • 5 of 9 lanes below or near the 5,600 goal
  • Technology incomplete after 18 months
  • Single decision-maker at ~400 dispatches/day

FP&A
Financial review — pull before going on-site
  • Pull and walk through the P&L
  • Understand how driver comp is structured and what the average paycheck looks like
  • What numbers does leadership actually manage to day-to-day in Expedited?
  • Truck depreciation — are we calculating it at average fleet rates or at the actual miles these trucks are running? EXP trucks run roughly twice the miles of a standard OTR unit. If we're using average rates, the cost is understated.
  • When new trucks were going into these lanes, maintenance was minimal. Now that older equipment is being used, what does the cost-per-mile trend look like over time?
  • Can we restate the P&L using depreciation adjusted for actual EXP mileage?
  • Is there a true loaded cost-per-mile that captures road assist calls, third-party shop time, and downtime — not just fuel and driver pay?
  • How is Driscoll's (CC 153) tracked separately, and does that P&L actually include the Mexico-side costs — partner carrier fees, trailer integrity issues, cross-border delays?

14
Equipment

How is truck depreciation factoring into the profitability of this operation? When Expedited launched, we were putting new trucks into these lanes — roughly the first six months saw almost no failures. We are not taking new trucks right now, and most of the maintenance issues we're seeing appear to be tied to older equipment aging out under higher-than-normal mileage loads.

Down equipment keeps coming up on calls. Are we tracking the most common failure types by lane or by truck? That data matters before making any decisions about how to address it.

One idea worth evaluating: could we stock basic replacement parts inside each unit? Drivers are home nightly, which means trucks are accessible. If the most common roadside issues are small and fixable, a basic parts kit could cut road assist calls. But this only makes sense if the issues are actually that type — if it's primarily larger mechanical failures, the kit doesn't help. Need to understand the breakdown before going further.

  • Are we tracking the most common failure types by lane or by truck?
  • Could we realistically resolve the most common breakdowns through on-site minor repairs?
  • What is the cost comparison between our internal shop and third-party shops for the issues we're seeing?

Gemba Walk — Scope & Rules
Week of April 13–18, 2026
Objective

Purpose of this walk is to see reality — not to fix. Validate how Expedited operations actually work versus how they are planned, documented, and described.

✅ In Scope
  • Expedited driver support & dispatch operations
  • End-to-end flow of a load within the relay model
  • Relay / swap execution and driver experience
  • AS400 / EPM screen usage and workarounds
  • Brief, debrief, and shift handoff mechanics
  • Information flow, interruptions, and rework cycles
🚫 Explicitly Out of Scope
  • Solution design
  • Technology requests or builds
  • Staffing decisions
  • Organizational changes
  • Any countermeasure commitments during the walk
Rules for the Walk — Own It
  • No fixing during observation
  • No judging individuals — judge the system
  • Write down what you see, not what you think it means
  • Facts first. Interpretation later.
  • Every observation is a question, not a conclusion
  • Ask "why does this happen?" not "who made this happen?"
What to Track Every Day
  • Information Flow: Patterns, constraints, flow stoppage, rework cycles
  • Load Lifecycle: Tender → assign → execute → complete → release
  • Relay/Swap & Driver Experience: Swap timing, driver comms, tablet usability, workarounds
  • AS400 Usage & Brief/Debrief: Screen usage, manual rework, handoff quality, status definitions
